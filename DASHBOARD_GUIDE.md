## ðŸ“š How to Interpret Ashtakavarga Tables

### **Understanding the Numbers**

1. **Sarvashtakavarga (SAV) Table**
   - Shows total benefic points (bindus) for each of the 12 houses
   - Total is sum of points contributed by 7 planets + ascendant
   - Always adds up to 337 in traditional system

2. **Bhinnashtakavarga (BAV) Table**
   - Shows individual points (0-8) that each planet contributes to each house
   - Helps assess specific effect of a particular planet on a house
   - Used for timing predictions and dasha analysis

### **General Point Evaluation Rules**

- **30+ points**: Auspicious - Very favorable results
- **25-29 points**: Average - Mixed results, no major troubles
- **Below 25 points**: Inauspicious - Potential challenges, requires attention

### **Planet Strength Rules**

- **5+ points**: Strong planet - Gives positive results
- **4 points**: Neutral planet - Mixed effects
- **0-3 points**: Weak planet - Limited positive results

### **Using for Predictions**

1. **Examine SAV**: Review total points in each house for overview
2. **Look for key comparisons**: Compare specific house pairs
3. **Analyze BAV**: Check individual planet scores in occupied houses
4. **Use for timing**: Combine SAV and BAV scores with planetary transits
